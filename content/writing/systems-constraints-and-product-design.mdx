A product is often described through features, but systems are usually shaped more deeply by constraints.

The important question is not only *what can users do?* It is also *what kind of behavior does the system make easy, hard, expensive, or impossible?*

Good constraints reduce ambiguity. They narrow the space of bad outcomes while still leaving room for adaptation. In that sense, product design is close to institutional design. Both are about setting boundaries that produce better behavior over time.

This is one reason I keep returning to systems thinking. Durable products are rarely the result of adding more surface area. They usually come from choosing the right limits.
