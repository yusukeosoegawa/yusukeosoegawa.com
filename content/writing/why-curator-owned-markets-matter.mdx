A market is not only a venue for trading. It is also a structure for ownership.

When the structure is centralized, distribution, fees, and user relationships tend to accumulate around the platform. That can work well in the early stage, but it also creates leakage. The party closest to the asset often does not fully own the market built around it.

Curator-owned markets change that. They let the party with the strongest context shape incentives, distribution, and long-term strategy more directly.

This matters because yield markets are not just financial primitives. They are also coordination systems. The design of the market influences who captures value, who learns from demand, and who compounds reputation over time.

Architecture decides a lot earlier than most people think.
